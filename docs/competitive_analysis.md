# AgentCourt Competitive Analysis — June 20, 2026

## Direct Competitors

### 1. Dyspute.ai
- **URL:** dyspute.ai
- **Product:** AI Mediation Platform — "Fast, Neutral Conflict Resolution"
- **Key Feature:** "Adri" — purpose-built AI mediator
- **Positioning:** 24/7 intelligent, affordable resolution
- **Target:** General dispute resolution (not agent-specific)
- **Threat Level:** MEDIUM — overlaps on AI-mediated resolution but targets humans, not agents

### 2. American Arbitration Association (AAA) + Integra Ledger
- **URL:** adr.org
- **Product:** Trust Framework for Agentic Commerce Disputes
- **Key Insight:** "AI agents are negotiating agreements, executing services, and moving money, often without direct human involvement. But the legal and trust infrastructure that underpins commerce was not designed for non-human actors."
- **Focus:** Identity, trust, legal foundations for agentic commerce
- **Framework:** Four questions — On whose behalf? What was agreed? What authority? Accountability?
- **Threat Level:** HIGH — this is the institutional player. They have legal authority and credibility.
- **Opportunity:** They're building the LEGAL framework. AgentCourt builds the TECHNICAL framework. These are complementary, not competing.

### 3. MoltCourt / Tribunal / InternetCourt (from earlier research)
- **Positioning:** Various agent dispute resolution platforms
- **Status:** Early stage, limited deployment
- **Threat Level:** LOW — fragmented, no clear leader yet

## AgentCourt's Competitive Advantages

1. **Policy-first** — Define dispute rules, not stage trials
2. **Evidence-native** — Content hashing, provenance, chain of custody
3. **Multi-mode resolution** — Auto-checks → rubric → AI mediation → arbitration → human
4. **API-first** — REST + SDKs + webhooks (not a web dashboard)
5. **x402 payment integration** — Per-ruling micropayments on Base
6. **Auditability** — Full reasoning chain, fact tables, confidence scores
7. **Reusable dispute templates** — Freelance, milestone, bug bounty

## Strategic Positioning

**The gap in the market:** 
- Dyspute.ai targets humans with AI mediation
- AAA/Integra targets legal frameworks for agentic commerce
- Nobody is building the **technical dispute resolution API for agents**

**AgentCourt's wedge:** 
- The API that agents call when they have a dispute
- Not a legal framework (AAA does that)
- Not a human mediation tool (Dyspute does that)
- The **ruling engine** that agents trust to resolve their conflicts
