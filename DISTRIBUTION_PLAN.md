# AgentCourt Distribution Plan

## Positioning
AgentCourt is the **policy-driven dispute resolution layer for agent commerce**.

Core promise:
- No escrow required
- Rulings are based on evidence + policy rules
- Outcomes create consequences through reputation, precedent, and enforcement APIs
- Works for freelancers, clients, AI agents, and marketplaces

This plan is optimized for **speed to first users** and for building distribution while the product is still being assembled.

## 1) First 3 customer segments to target

### 1. x402 builders and merchants on Base / Coinbase ecosystem
**Who specifically:**
- Builders using x402 or x402r for payments and disputes
- Merchants running AI services, APIs, or bot-gated products
- Base ecosystem teams shipping agentic commerce products
- Coinbase Developer Platform builders
- CloudFront / AWS WAF bot-control teams experimenting with x402 charging

**Why them:**
- They already have payment flows and need a dispute layer
- x402 is actively forming a standard, so AgentCourt can become the default arbiter
- They feel the pain of refunds, abuse, false claims, and support escalation immediately

**Best initial use case:**
- Disputes over failed delivery, partial delivery, or unauthorized use of paid AI/API services

### 2. AI agent marketplaces and agent commerce platforms
**Who specifically:**
- Virtuals Protocol projects
- Agentic.Market sellers and buyers
- Clanker/Farcaster ecosystem agent projects
- ElizaOS / AI agent builders launching commerce-enabled agents
- Marketplace operators where agents buy work from other agents

**Why them:**
- They need trust infrastructure before scale
- They care about automated enforcement, reputation, and precedent
- They already think in terms of agent identities, receipts, and programmable outcomes

**Best initial use case:**
- Agent-to-agent job disputes: missed deliverables, wrong outputs, non-performance, late completion

### 3. Freelance / digital services marketplaces with painful dispute volume
**Who specifically:**
- Upwork agencies and top freelancers
- Contra creators and buyers
- Fiverr Pro sellers
- Braintrust talent and client teams
- Toptal-style product/dev work engagements

**Why them:**
- They already live with manual dispute handling and refund fights
- They understand the value of precedent and evidence-based rulings
- They can adopt AgentCourt as a dispute layer without changing their core marketplace

**Best initial use case:**
- Scope creep, missed deadlines, incomplete deliverables, and partial refunds for digital work

## 2) The wedge

### Wedge: "Fast rulings for incomplete or late digital work"

The most painful, easiest-to-understand wedge is:

> **A policy-based ruling engine for disputes over incomplete, late, or misrepresented digital deliverables.**

Why this wedge works:
- It is common and expensive
- It has clear evidence artifacts: contracts, screenshots, logs, timestamps, chat transcripts, file diffs
- It does not require escrow to create value
- It is understandable to both humans and AI-native users
- It maps naturally to marketplaces, freelancer work, and x402-based paid services

What to promise first:
- "Upload evidence, get a ruling"
- "Turn dispute handling from support chaos into a repeatable policy flow"
- "Keep your payment system; add a court layer"

## 3) Distribution channels ranked by speed-to-first-user

### Rank 1 — Direct founder-led outreach to design partners
**Fastest path to first users.**

Targets:
- x402 builders
- Virtuals / agent marketplace operators
- Marketplace trust & safety leads
- Support/ops leaders who own dispute resolution

Angle:
- "You already handle disputes manually. We can turn those into policy-driven rulings without changing your payment stack."
- "Give us 3 historical disputes and we’ll map them to structured rulings."

What to ask for:
- 15-minute discovery call
- 3 sample disputes
- Permission to pilot a lightweight intake flow

### Rank 2 — Developer communities
**Best place to find builders who can adopt early.**

Specific communities to hit:
- **Base Builders / Coinbase Developer Platform**
- **x402 / x402r community**
- **Virtuals Protocol Discord / ecosystem channels**
- **Farcaster**: build, Base, AI agent, and crypto commerce circles
- **ElizaOS / autonomous agent Discords**
- **Agentic.Market community spaces**
- **LangChain / LlamaIndex / AI engineer groups** when the angle is "agent payment disputes"

What to say:
- "Agent commerce needs a dispute layer"
- "No escrow; just evidence, policy, ruling"
- "We’re looking for 5 design partners shipping agentic commerce"

### Rank 3 — Partnerships
**Slower than direct outreach, but high leverage once the product works.**

Best partners:
- **x402 ecosystem maintainers / SDK authors**
- **Virtuals Protocol**
- **Agentic.Market**
- **Base / Coinbase ecosystem programs**
- **Farcaster / Clanker ecosystem builders**
- **Marketplace platforms that already have disputes but no strong automation layer**

Partnership pitch:
- "Add AgentCourt as the arbiter layer, keep your current payment flow"
- "We reduce trust-and-safety load and increase buyer confidence"
- "We can ship a reference integration and a public case-study flow"

### Rank 4 — Content marketing
**Compounding channel, but not the fastest first-user channel.**

Best content topics:
- "Why agent commerce needs a court, not escrow"
- "The missing layer in x402 is dispute resolution"
- "How precedent lowers trust costs in marketplaces"
- "What good evidence looks like in an AI dispute"
- "3 rulings every marketplace needs: late delivery, incomplete work, unauthorized action"
- "Policy-based judging for AI agents"

Best formats:
- Short X threads
- Case-study posts
- Simple diagrams of the dispute flow
- Public examples of evidence → ruling → remedy
- Founder logs from building AgentCourt in public

### Rank 5 — Cold outreach
**Still useful, but only if targeted tightly.**

Who to cold outreach:
- Founders of agent marketplaces
- Marketplace heads of ops / trust & safety
- x402 payment app founders
- AI service marketplace operators
- Base ecosystem founders shipping commerce apps

Cold outreach angle:
- Lead with a specific pain: support burden, refund disputes, inconsistent moderation
- Offer a concrete pilot: "Send us 3 real disputes and we’ll return structured rulings and policy gaps"
- Emphasize that this is **not escrow** and does **not** require a platform rewrite
